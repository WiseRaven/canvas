For me, this doesn’t feel like a business. When friends and clients come to visit my print studio, they often tell me it feels more like a home. I’m pleased to know that I’ve created a warm and welcoming space, one that reflects my personality and makes my customers feel comfortable and relaxed about a process that is often unknown and sometimes daunting.

After many hectic years of a life filled with paperwork, unfinished projects, and a seemingly never-ending scramble to get myself out of some type of mess, I am enjoying the fruits of a much simpler life. I’m now creating a life where I can give back to others and to the wider community through this creative venture.

It has been a gradual process over time, as I grew, I started understanding what I needed in my life, and began to let go of what I didn't. It was a simplification down to, “what is it I enjoy the most?”. I filtered through what was worth cherishing, and which memories I wanted to really hold onto.

When I put my studio together with all of my artworks and prints, I wanted it to be a reflection of me - with lots of colour, filled with the things I love and the sentimental memories I cherish.

Helping my customers to preserve and cherish their special memories is a huge part of why I do what I do, it makes the whole process worthwhile to me. It brings out my sentimental side because I know how special the piece will be to the person.

My work allows me to be really generous and I love that I am able to help bring people’s imagery to life. Creating a product that is made with love and intention, and offering genuine and honest service, makes me feel so happy to do what I do.

In the greater scheme of things, it’s not making a huge impact in the world, but for me it’s the little things in life. Helping another person create something that is meaningful and precious to them - this is what makes all the difference to me.
